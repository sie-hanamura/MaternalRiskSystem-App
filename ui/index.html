üîç How Maternal Risk Assessment Works

The Maternal Risk Assessment System uses machine learning trained on 986 maternal health records from the Municipal Health Office Bay, Laguna, spanning 2019-2025. This comprehensive dataset captures diverse pregnancy outcomes across rural Filipino communities.

The system analyzes clinical measurements including BMI, blood pressure, blood sugar, and hemoglobin levels to predict pregnancy risk levels (Low, Moderate, or High). It supports healthcare workers in identifying high-risk pregnancies requiring specialist referral to OB-GYN facilities.

All recommendations are based on WHO, ACOG, and Philippine DOH maternal health guidelines. The system operates completely offline, making it ideal for rural barangay health stations with limited internet connectivity.

Two models are available: a Full Model (5 features) when lab results are available, and a Basic Model (3 features) for preliminary screening when labs are not accessible.

üìã Assessment Process Flow

STEP 1: Health Worker Inputs

Age, weight, height, blood pressure measurements
Optional: blood sugar, hemoglobin (if lab results available)

STEP 2: Automatic Calculations

BMI computed from weight/height ratio
Feature validation and normalization applied

STEP 3: ML Model Analysis

Full Model (5 features) if labs available
Basic Model (3 features) if no labs

STEP 4: Risk Prediction

Low/Moderate/High classification generated
Confidence level and probability breakdown calculated

STEP 5: Clinical Recommendations

Evidence-based care guidelines provided
Referral instructions if specialist care needed
